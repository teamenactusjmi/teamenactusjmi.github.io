TABDEEL

Urdu, Change
You can change the world again, instead of protecting yourself from it.

Enactus Jamia Millia Islamia’s flagship project, “Tabdeel” standing true to its name aims at delivering a revolutionary change concerning two of India’s major problems namely waste management and welfare of underprivileged communities.

THE WASTE REVOLUTION

Tabdeel focuses on creating means of self sustained livelihood through the ropes of successful waste management. Biodegradable waste is utilised in composting pits to generate minimum waste reduction and in turn utilising that converted waste as organic fertilizers. In the case of non biodegradable wastes, steps have been taken to make use of shredding machines to reduce the size of the waste generated and in turn re using it into an assortment of other marketable products.

OVERVIEW

To actualize the idea of ‘Zero Waste’, Enactus JMI decided to build contact with two nearby communities. Enactus JMI started with educating the rag pickers in our neighboring areas and those working within our university about the importance of segregating waste. For our business model, this segregated waste was developed into products that turned out providing entrepreneurial opportunity to another community namely the Sambhal women who reside in the locality of Batla House and are skilled in the art of crochet. The Sambhal women were also guided on proper waste management and its segregation by the Horticulture Department of Jamia Millia Islamia by the use of composting pits to help deal with biodegradable waste. Also, Enactus JMI held computer sessions for the children the rag picker community and the sambhal community to impart the basic knowledge of computers and its importance in today’s world.

GOALS

  • Employment generation for the rag-picker community and underprivileged women.
  • Proper arrangement for waste management in the institution and commercial complexes.
  • Materializing the Zero Waste philosophy, i.e. to recycle and reuse plastic waste, so that there is no need to make new plastic, and to use the waste produced judiciously.

    • MEANS OF EMPOWERMENT

  • Arrangement of digital literacy for the children involved in rag picking by enrolling them intolocal government schools and organizing computer classes for them in the extra      computer labs of Faculty of Engineering and Technology, Jamia Millia Islamia.
  • Engagement of Sambhal women in compliance with the Govt. issued USTTAD scheme for their employment and economic growth.
  • Community members were also engaged in Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana is a skill enhancement scheme started by Government of India. This is an umbrella scheme of skill India.

    • CONCERNS



    Social

  • Elevating the conditions of the rag-picker community. Also, addressing the plight of underprivileged women.

  • Helping the communities combat the issues of unjustified and irregular rates of work, temporary abodes and unhygienic living conditions.

  • Catering to the need of Mussart Ji’s plight by providing skill enhancement training to her children.

  • In accordance with the USTTAD scheme issued by the Ministry of Minority Affairs, initiatives have been taken for the training and skill development of the Sambhal women. Their products are being sold at Delhi haat.

    • Economical

  • The Horticulture Department of Jamia Millia Islamia guided the women of the Sambhal community in the much needed ropes of successful waste management by training them in making composting pits for biodegradable wastes.

  • The biodegradable waste is in turn supplied to the Jamia nursery as vermi compost.

  • Shredding machines have been incorporated to deal with non biodegradable wastes . The shredding machine works by reducing the plastic waste size into smaller flakes to further increase its functionality for use into various new marketable products.

  • Establishing compression moulding machines to make use of the shredded plastic to make new products out of them.

  • Collaboration with NGO’s namely Umeed and Chintan to buy the products generated to garner the needed revenue.

  • Evading the involvement of middlemen as well as miscellaneous costs of transportation, storage etc. to ensure that the generated income is solely earned by the community members involved.

    • Environmental

  • The women of the Sambhal community have been provided the needed guidance and training in making composting pits for the biodegradable waste generated by the Horticulture department of the university.

  • Educating the cleaning staff of the university about the importance of sorting the waste into biodegradable and non-biodegradable.

  • Utilization of non-biodegradable plastic waste like milk packets by collecting them from hostels and canteens and turning them into durable products such as mats and bags.
