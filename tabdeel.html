TABDEEL

Urdu, Change
You can change the world again, instead of protecting yourself from it.

One of the flagship projects of Enactus - Jamia Millia Islamia, “Tabdeel” aims at bringing forth a transformation with regard to two major societal concerns namely waste management and welfare of underprivileged communities.

The project aims to create a means of sustainable livelihood through proper waste management and utilization of plastic bags for manufacturing products for the homeless such as cheap mattresses etc.

The focal point of Project Tabdeel is curbing the menace of waste management. Ranked amongst the top ten waste generators in the world, India suffers from a lack of technology and infrastructure needed to manage as well as reutilize this humongous quantity of waste. In the existing model in our country, collection and disposal of waste does take place at the most basic level, through the community of rag pickers. Exploitation of the underprivileged by the hands of those in power is a major crisis as rag pickers are not provided with regular and justified rates of work, and due to lack of proper economic support, their temporary abodes and unhygienic living conditions strip them of their basic human rights.

Through project Tabdeel, we at Enactus JMI, strive to bring to life the idea of ‘Zero Waste’ by engaging this community of rag pickers along with the community of Sambhal women residing in Batla House. This philosophy of Zero Waste encourages the use of resources to their fullest capacity by designing products that take an entire life cycle into consideration.

The team has taken the responsibility of educating the staff working in Jamia Millia Islamia about the importance of segregating waste. Plastic waste generated in and around the university is collected by the rag picker community and developed into products that provide entrepreneurial opportunity to the Sambhal women who are skilled in the art of crochet. Start-ups like Pom-Pom Pvt. Ltd. working with Enactus JMI also help to provide the right price for other plastic and waste products sold by the rag picker community, thus creating a proper chain of management.

The goal of Enactus JMI is ethical, economical and efficient, aiming to guide people to change their lifestyles and practices to emulate sustainable natural cycles, where all discarded materials are designed to become resources for others to use.



Social

  • Elevating the conditions of the rag picker community and combating the issue of unjustified and irregular rates of work.
  • Addressing the plight of homeless and underprivileged women by engaging them in reviving the art of crochet.
  • Arrangement of digital literacy for the children involved in rag picking by enrolling them into local government schools and organizing computer classes for them in the computer labs of Faculty of Engineering and Technology, Jamia Millia Islamia.

    • Economical

  • Creating a link between the rag picker community and start-ups such as Pom-Pom Pvt. Ltd. to help in waste management, assisting in transportation of, and paying the right price for recyclables.
  • Collaborating with NGOs such as Shikhar to buy these products, thus generating revenue and donating much of it to the homeless.
  • Evading the involvement of middlemen as well as miscellaneous costs of transportation, storage etc. to ensure that the generated income is solely earned by the community members involved.

    • Environmental

  • Utilization of non-biodegradable plastic waste by collecting it from hostels and college premises, turning it into durable products such as mats and bags.
  • Educating the cleaning staff of Jamia Millia Islamia about the importance of segregation of waste into biodegradable and non-biodegradable.
