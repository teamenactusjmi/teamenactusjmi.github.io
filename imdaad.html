IMDAAD

Urdu, Help
One of the most important things you can do on this earth is to let people know they are not alone.

The meaning of Imdaad is "to help" and we at Enactus JMI seek to bring together communities from two different states in India, by building a model that works on codependency and supporting each others' livelihood. The project is a sustainable business model stimulated by a naturally crafted healthy and refreshing drink known as ‚ÄòKerala Kan‚Äô.

Kudumbashree is a community of women in Kerala, hailing from socio-economically deprived part of the society, working as a self help group. The other community involved in this project is Marham, an NGO advocating the cause of homeless people. It attempts to reintegrate the rehabilitated youth into the society through vocational training and education.

Nannari roots, available in Kerala, are medicinal herbs with effective cooling and body purifying properties. The syrup from these roots is prepared by the women of Kudumbashree community and the final product is synthesized by NGO Marham using either milk or water.

Delhi, with extreme conditions in the warmer months proves to be an ideal market for such health drinks. Kerala Kan has maximum health benefits as it happens to be a 100% natural drink.

The engagement of Kudumbashree women of Kerala and rehabilitated youth from the city of Delhi in creating and manufacturing Kerala Kan has been an effective measure in the development of both the communities.
