IRTIQA

To grow, evolve and create is the essence of humanity.

Project 'Irtiqa' (Urdu for evolution) is a socio-entrepreneurial project of Enactus Jamia Millia Islamia based in the vicinity of Jamia Nagar, Okhla and is laid down on the objectives put down by the National Bamboo Mission of India and the United Nations' goals for sustainable development. With a demand of an estimated 26 million metric tonnes, the industry is expected to significantly grow further in the near future making it a buzzing ground for small entrepreneurs. Being the second largest producer of bamboo all over the world, the bamboo industry in India is a booming hot-spot creating an approximate profit of 9,000 crores for the country.

MISSION

The project aims to create an alternative to the depleting scope of business for the local community of wood workers by introducing them to the art of bamboo handicraft creating for them a more sustainable business alternative as bamboo is an easier source of raw material and is more environment friendly.

OVERVIEW

Living in the nearby Batla house, the community faces immense financial hardships in their day-to-day activities affecting their quality of life rampantly the market for wooden products is gradually becoming unpopular due to the exploitation by the middlemen and the unrealistic high range prices. This is where we took the opportunity to help the wood-workers community from Sambhal, Uttar Pradesh switch to the bamboo craft business cutting them off from the exploitation and the under-paid slavery that they were subjected to. Bamboo can be grown in almost any climatic condition and is already largely grown in the Jamia Nagar area making it even more suitable for a smooth supply chain and low cost raw material leading to rise in the actual profit.

GOALS

  • Revival of the depleting business opportunities for woodworkers in the Sambhal community by introducing them to bamboo as a more viable alternative for wood and simultaneously contributing in the sustainability goals remains as the major objectives of project Irtiqa.

  • Assuring that there is least interference of the middlemen who take up 50-55% of the total profit and cut off the production cost increasing the final profit by exposing the community to larger markets. As a result of this initiative, the income of the artisans improved by 52% paralleled to the wood work they used to do before.

  • Skill the workers in the craft of making bamboo-products while also ensuring safety measures are taken with provision of gloves, apron and safety mask to the manufacturers.

  • Promotion of digital literacy and imparting life-skills of digital marketing on e-commerce websites, digital payment, enabling them with skills to manage inventory, handling logistics like delivery and packaging on their own.

  • Bamboo absorbs greenhouse gases 5 times more efficiently than wood and thus proves to be a ‘Climate-smart Solution’ hence promoting sensitization regarding the alarming environmental concerns.

  • Bamboo emits 30% more oxygen and absorbs substantial amount of carbon emission hence making our project at par with the 2018 Paris Summit’s objective to limit the global warming level at 1.5°C. The remains of the bamboo are used up to manufacture edible products such as pickles thus also encouraging the policy of zero-waste in production.

    • EMPOWERMENT

  • Provision of digital literacy to the community members has contributed largely in rendering them self-dependant and making them at par with the technological advancements while also exposing them to new areas of marketing and business.

  • The skills learnt during the 120 hrs by Livelihood and Business Incubator established by Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (CIE), under MSME has enabled them to produce good quality bamboo products. By collaborating with CIE, we were able to cut back on the cost of raw material hence providing access to inexhaustible source of raw material to them without any interference from a middleman.

  • Team leaders from Sambhal community were identified and trained in the management and logistics involved in digital marketing, which gave them exposure to new sales area.

    • IMPACT

  • Raw material for our community is now available at cheaper price increasing their income exponentially. Also, we have actively spread awareness regarding ‘Green Building’ which utilizes bamboo as an alternative to hardwood.

  • The bamboo products manufactured were sold in local markets like INA Haat and e-commerce websites such as Amazon and Enactus JMI Store.

  • The income for community has grown significantly by Rs 5,200/- per month for each member which is being channelized towards better education, better living standards and adopting a sustainable way of earning.

  • In addition to this, Enactus JMI have helped the children of the community with their academics and social inhibitions.



    • NEEDS

  • We seek to fulfill the three aspects of need the social and the economical needs of the community and the environmental needs of our planet.





    • Social

  • Freeing the wood-worker community from the clutches of the middlemen and helping them switch to better alternative for sustainable business hence uplifting their lives and making them more skilled.

    • Economical

  • Providing them with greater skills of entrepreneurship to make self independent while also hiking up their profit margin by cutting the middlemen cost and exposing them to a larger digital market as well.

    • Environmental

  • Switching to bamboo in order to encourage it as an alternative to wood hence promoting green cover besides encouraging zero-waste production by using the remains in producing edible items.
