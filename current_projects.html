OUR CURRENT PROJECTS

Project Tabdeel

Enactus Jamia Millia Islamia’s flagship project, “Tabdeel” standing true to its name aims at delivering a revolutionary change concerning two of India’s major problems namely waste management and welfare of underprivileged communities.

Tabdeel focuses on creating means of self sustained livelihood through the ropes of successful waste management. Biodegradable waste is utilised in composting pits to generate minimum waste reduction and in turn utilising that converted waste as organic fertilizers. In the case of non biodegradable wastes, steps have been taken to make use of shredding machines to reduce the size of the waste generated and in turn re using it into an assortment of other marketable products.

Project IMDAAD

The women of Jasola community, hailing from socio-economically deprived part of the society are targeted through the Project ‘Imdaad’ which aims to support them by generating revenue and essential livelihood standards. This project is a sustainable business model simulated by a naturally crafted healthy and refreshing drink. The model is based upon a supply-chain system which primarily functions from Kerala to Delhi.

The Indian Sarsaparilla roots, a medicinal herb with effective cooling and body purifying properties, are used to prepare the drink. The syrup is extracted from the roots and produced by the Jasola community. It has an improved shelf life because the drink is now being tetra packed. The process of manufacturing the syrup commences with the plucking of roots followed by washing, soaking, boiling and finally extracting the syrup from the roots while being mindful of the hygiene. It is then synthesised by the Jasola community using either milk or water.The community also plays an integral role in selling of the drink through the stalls which are setup in college festivals or corporate events.
